# Agentic Systems

Agentic systems refer to autonomous AI systems capable of performing tasks with minimal human intervention, particularly discussed in finance and research contexts. These systems often involve self-improving agents, such as those managing financial portfolios or automating post-training workflows in machine learning. Examples include Fere AI’s self-improving trading agents and Hugging Face’s ML Intern, an open-source AI agent designed for automating LLM post-training processes [[sources/slack-digest-ai-channel-updates-april-2026]].

The concept is significant for research and industry applications, as it highlights the growing integration of autonomous AI in domains like finance, where agents can manage complex tasks such as portfolio optimization, and in weather forecasting, where AI-driven systems like NOAA’s AIGFS demonstrate efficiency gains and improved accuracy. These developments underscore the potential of agentic systems to transform workflows, reduce computational costs, and extend predictive capabilities in critical fields [[sources/slack-digest-ai-channel-updates-april-2026]].
