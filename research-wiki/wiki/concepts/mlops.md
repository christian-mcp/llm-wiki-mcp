# MLOps

MLOps refers to the practice of operationalizing machine learning systems, a key concern for infrastructure work within the MCP Research Team. It encompasses the design, deployment, and maintenance of scalable machine learning pipelines, with a focus on ensuring reliability, reproducibility, and integration with production systems. This concept is particularly relevant to JP Sada’s role as Infrastructure & Data Engineering Lead, where MLOps is highlighted as a critical area for managing data leakage, backtest integrity, and alternative data ingestion [[sources/mcp-research-team-paper-assignment-profiles.md]].

The importance of MLOps in this research context lies in its role as a bridge between machine learning experimentation and real-world deployment. By addressing challenges such as LLM temporal leakage, lookahead bias, and point-in-time data handling, MLOps ensures that models remain robust and aligned with infrastructure requirements. This is especially critical for research involving complex data pipelines, where infrastructure oversight directly impacts the validity of results [[sources/mcp-research-team-paper-assignment-profiles.md]].

MLOps connects to related concepts and entities, including [[entities/jp-sada]], who oversees production systems end-to-end, and [[concepts/data-leakage]], a key concern in infrastructure-adjacent research. It also intersects with [[facts/jp-sada-infrastructure-oversight]], which details the team’s focus on ensuring data integrity and system reliability in machine learning workflows [[sources/mcp-research-team-paper-assignment-profiles.md]].
