# Agentic AI

Agentic AI refers to a research area focused on autonomous agents and multi-agent systems designed to perform complex tasks. It is central to the work of Christian Marin and Carlos within the MCP Research Team, with applications spanning LLM deployment, reinforcement learning, and agent frameworks [[sources/mcp-research-team-paper-assignment-profiles.md]]. This concept emphasizes the development of systems where multiple agents collaborate or compete to achieve goals, often involving local LLMs and tools like Clawdbot for orchestration. Christian Marin’s focus leans toward infrastructure and deployment, while Carlos applies agentic AI principles to finance and investing, such as anomaly detection and signal blending [[sources/mcp-research-team-paper-assignment-profiles.md]].

The significance of Agentic AI in this research context lies in its potential to enhance decision-making through decentralized, adaptive systems. Christian Marin’s work on LLM pipeline orchestration and on-prem inference, combined with Carlos’s exploration of LLMs for quant research, highlights its relevance to both technical deployment and financial applications. This duality connects Agentic AI to broader concepts like [[concepts/llm-pipeline-orchestration]] and [[concepts/mlops]], as well as entities such as [[entities/qwen3]] and [[entities/clawdbot]] [[sources/mcp-research-team-paper-assignment-profiles.md]].

Agentic AI intersects with multiple research threads within the team, including LLM deployment, multi-agent reinforcement learning, and finance-specific applications. It is closely tied to [[entities/christian-marin]] and [[entities/carlos]], who collaborate on routing papers related to agentic systems. This concept also links to [[concepts/spatial-temporal-forecasting]] and [[facts/christian-marin-llm-deployment]], reflecting its integration with both technical and domain-specific challenges [[sources/mcp-research-team-paper-assignment-profiles.md]].
