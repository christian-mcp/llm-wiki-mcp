# LLM Pipeline Orchestration

LLM Pipeline Orchestration refers to the process of managing and deploying large language models (LLMs) in production environments, a focus area for Christian Marin within the MCP Research Team. This concept involves coordinating the technical infrastructure required to operationalize LLMs, including tools like Clawdbot, frameworks for multi-agent systems, and considerations for on-prem inference and local LLM deployment [[sources/mcp-research-team-paper-assignment-profiles.md]].

This concept is critical for advancing research in agentic AI and LLM deployment, as it addresses challenges in scaling models for real-world applications. It intersects with MLOps and data pipeline management, ensuring that LLMs function reliably in environments requiring temporal leakage prevention, point-in-time data handling, and alternative data integration. The orchestration process also ties into infrastructure priorities outlined by JP Sada, emphasizing the need for robust systems to support both model deployment and research integrity [[sources/mcp-research-team-paper-assignment-profiles.md]].

The concept connects to related pages such as [[entities/christian-marin]], who leads efforts in this domain, and [[concepts/agentic-ai]], which overlaps with LLM deployment strategies. It also links to [[concepts/mlops]] for infrastructure considerations and [[entities/qwen3]] as an example of local LLMs requiring orchestration. These connections highlight the interdisciplinary nature of pipeline orchestration, bridging model development, infrastructure, and application-specific requirements [[sources/mcp-research-team-paper-assignment-profiles.md]].
