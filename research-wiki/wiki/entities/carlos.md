# Carlos

Carlos is a specialist in applying large language models (LLMs) to investing and quantitative research, with a focus on anomalies, factor models, and signal blending. His work spans agentic systems for finance, meta-models, ensemble methods, and research methodology, including cross-sectional signals and approaches influenced by Lopez de Prado. He collaborates closely with [[entities/christian-marin]] on agentic AI topics, though his emphasis leans toward financial signals and modeling rather than infrastructure or deployment [[sources/mcp-research-team-paper-assignment-profiles]].

In the MCP research team’s paper assignment framework, Carlos is the primary routing target for research involving LLMs-for-investing, anomalies, and factor/meta-models. His role notes highlight overlaps with Christian Marin on agentic AI, but with a finance-heavy focus, distinguishing him from Christian’s infrastructure/deployment emphasis. This specialization aligns with concepts like [[concepts/agentic-ai]] and [[concepts/llm-pipeline-orchestration]], and he is connected to related entities such as [[entities/christian-marin]] and [[entities/qwen3]] through collaborative projects [[sources/mcp-research-team-paper-assignment-profiles]].
