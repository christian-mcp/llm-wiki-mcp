# Rafael Gallegos — Weather × Commodities (PhD)

Rafael Gallegos is a PhD researcher specializing in the intersection of weather and commodities, with a focus on applying machine learning to crop yield forecasting, satellite data analysis, and spatial-temporal modeling. His work involves leveraging remote sensing and climate signals to improve agricultural outcomes, as highlighted in the [[sources/mcp-research-team-paper-assignment-profiles]] document. He is embedded in the weather research pipeline, with a particular emphasis on agricultural data and the [[entities/berkeley-weather-pipeline]].

Gallegos’ expertise spans satellite remote sensing, spatial-temporal forecasting, and the integration of climate signals into commodity market analysis. His role involves starting research assignments in weather, agriculture, and satellite-related domains, expanding outward as projects evolve. This aligns with the [[concepts/spatial-temporal-forecasting]] and [[concepts/data-leakage]] areas of focus within the team’s infrastructure, as noted in [[sources/mcp-research-team-paper-assignment-profiles]].
