# Berkeley Weather Pipeline

The [[Berkeley Weather Pipeline]] is a research infrastructure mentioned in [[entities/rafael-gallegos]]'s work on weather and agricultural data, specifically within the context of his PhD research focused on weather-commodities interactions. It is described as part of a broader effort to integrate spatial-temporal forecasting, satellite remote sensing, and crop yield machine learning models to analyze climate signals and their impact on agricultural outcomes. This infrastructure is positioned as a tool for processing and analyzing complex environmental data, with applications in both weather prediction and commodity market analysis [[sources/mcp-research-team-paper-assignment-profiles]].

The pipeline is closely tied to [[entities/rafael-gallegos]]'s research focus, which includes crop yield ML, satellite data, and climate signals. It is part of a larger ecosystem of tools and methodologies used in the MCP research team’s work, intersecting with concepts like [[concepts/spatial-temporal-forecasting]] and [[concepts/data-leakage]]. While specific technical details of the pipeline are not elaborated in the source, its mention in Rafael Gallegos’ role notes highlights its importance in the team’s exploration of weather-agricultural data linkages [[sources/mcp-research-team-paper-assignment-profiles]].
